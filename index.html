Brown Plant Hopper (BPH)

BPH adults and nymphs suck plant sap from the base of the rice plant. Heavily infested crops dry up, forming patches known as 'hopper burn'.

Scientific Action Plan:

1
Cultural Control

Drain the field for 3-4 days during the early stage of infestation. Avoid excessive use of Nitrogen fertilizers.

2
Chemical Composition (Generic)

If Economic Threshold Level (ETL) crosses 5-10 hoppers/hill: Spray Imidacloprid 17.8% SL @ 40 ml/acre OR Pymetrozine 50% WG @ 120 g/acre.

Ask your local dealer for these generic salts. We do not endorse brands.
